# Privacy Policy & Terms of Service — What You Need on Your Site or Product

## Why These Documents Are Required

If you sell anything online or run any kind of web presence, you likely need both a **Privacy Policy** and **Terms of Service**. These aren't optional niceties — in many jurisdictions they are legally required, and payment processors (Stripe, PayPal) and app stores (Google, Apple) often require them to approve your account.

---

## Privacy Policy

### What It Is
A document that tells users what personal data you collect, how you use it, and their rights over it.

### When You Need One
- **Always**, if you:
  - Have a website with any analytics (Google Analytics counts)
  - Collect email addresses (newsletter, waitlist, purchase notification)
  - Process payments (payment processors collect data on your behalf)
  - Use cookies or tracking pixels

### What to Include
1. **What data you collect**: Names, emails, payment info, usage data, IP addresses
2. **How you collect it**: Forms, cookies, third-party processors
3. **Why you collect it**: Fulfilling orders, sending updates, analytics
4. **Who you share it with**: Payment processors, email platforms, analytics tools
5. **How long you keep it**: Retention policy
6. **User rights**: How to request deletion, opt out, etc.
7. **Contact information**: How to reach you with privacy questions

### Key Laws That May Apply

| Law | Applies When | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| GDPR (EU) | Any EU resident visits or buys | Explicit consent, right to erasure, data processing agreement |
| CCPA (California) | Selling to California residents, revenue > $25M OR > 100K records | Right to know, opt out of data sale, non-discrimination |
| COPPA | Any users under 13 | Parental consent required, special handling |
| CAN-SPAM | Email marketing to US | Unsubscribe link, physical address, honest subject lines |

**If you sell to anyone in the EU, you need GDPR-compliant policies.** This is not optional and fines can be severe.

### How to Create One
- **Free generators**: Termly (`termly.io`), iubenda, PrivacyPolicies.com — generate a basic policy based on your answers
- **Paid/custom**: An attorney can draft one tailored to your specific data practices
- **Do not copy/paste** someone else's privacy policy. It must reflect your actual practices.

## Terms of Service (ToS) / Terms and Conditions (T&C)

### What It Is
The contract between you and your users/buyers. Defines the rules of engagement.

### What to Include

1. **Acceptance of terms**: By using the product, the user agrees to these terms
2. **Description of services**: What you offer, what you don't
3. **User eligibility**: Age requirements, geographic restrictions
4. **License and IP**: You own your product; users get a license
5. **Prohibited conduct**: What users cannot do
6. **Payment terms**: Prices, billing, what happens on failed payments
7. **Refund policy**: Your policy, stated clearly
8. **Disclaimer of warranties**: "As is" — you are not guaranteeing outcomes
9. **Limitation of liability**: Cap on your liability (typically the amount paid)
10. **Governing law**: Which state's laws govern disputes
11. **Dispute resolution**: Arbitration clause, class action waiver (optional but common)
12. **Changes to terms**: How you notify users of updates
13. **Contact information**

### Specific Clauses for AI/Bot Products

- **AI output disclaimer**: Outputs are generated by AI and may be inaccurate. Not professional advice of any kind (legal, medical, financial).
- **No reliance on outputs**: User assumes all risk from acting on AI outputs.
- **Prompt confidentiality**: If your system prompt is proprietary, state that users cannot reverse-engineer or republish it.
- **Acceptable use**: Explicitly prohibit using your bot for spam, illegal activity, harassment, deception.

## Refund Policy

Digital products typically have more restrictive refund policies than physical goods. Common approaches:
- **No refunds** on digital downloads (must be clearly stated before purchase)
- **14-day money back** for subscriptions (builds trust, reduces chargebacks)
- **Store credit** instead of cash refunds

Stripe and PayPal give buyers chargeback rights regardless of your stated policy. A clear, fair refund policy reduces chargebacks.

## Cookie Banner / Consent Management

If you have a website with analytics or advertising cookies and any EU visitors, you need a **cookie consent banner** that:
- Informs users what cookies you use
- Gets opt-in consent for non-essential cookies (analytics, marketing)
- Allows users to withdraw consent

Free tools: Termly, Cookiebot (limited free tier), CookieYes.
