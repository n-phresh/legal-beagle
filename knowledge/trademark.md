# Trademark — Protecting Your Name and Brand

## What Is a Trademark?

A trademark is a word, phrase, logo, or symbol that identifies the source of goods or services. It tells the world "this came from me."

Examples of things you might trademark:
- The name of your bot (e.g. "Hermes", "Legal Beagle")
- A logo or visual mark
- A tagline or slogan
- A product or service name

## What a Trademark Does NOT Protect

- The content inside your bot (that's copyright)
- The underlying code (also copyright, or potentially a patent)
- Your business entity name (that's state-level registration, not a trademark)

## Trademark vs. Copyright vs. Business Name

| | Trademark | Copyright | Business Name |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protects | Brand identifiers | Creative works | Legal entity name |
| Where registered | USPTO (federal) | U.S. Copyright Office | State Secretary of State |
| Automatic? | Partially (common law) | Yes, at creation | No — you file |

## Common Law vs. Federal Registration

**Common law trademark** — You automatically get some rights just by using a mark in commerce (selling things under that name). You'd use the ™ symbol. Limited geographic protection.

**Federal registration** — You file with the USPTO and get nationwide protection, the right to use ®, the ability to block imports of infringing goods, and standing to sue in federal court. Much stronger.

## The ™ vs. ® Symbols

- ™ — Unregistered mark. You're claiming it's yours, but you haven't filed with the USPTO.
- ® — Federally registered. You filed and were approved. Using ® before approval is illegal.

## Key Steps to Register a Trademark (Federal)

1. **Search the USPTO database** — Before filing, search TESS (Trademark Electronic Search System) at `tess2.uspto.gov` to make sure your mark isn't already taken.
2. **Choose your filing basis** — "Use in Commerce" (you're already selling) or "Intent to Use" (you plan to).
3. **Identify your goods/services class** — Trademarks are filed under one of 45 international classes. AI bots/software typically fall under Class 9 (software) or Class 42 (software as a service / technology services).
4. **File via TEAS** — The Trademark Electronic Application System at `USPTO.gov`. As of 2024, the base filing fee is $250–$350 per class depending on the form used.
5. **Wait for examination** — USPTO examines the application (typically 8–12 months). They may issue an Office Action (a request to clarify or reject).
6. **Publication** — If approved, the mark is published for opposition (30 days for others to object).
7. **Registration** — If no opposition, you receive a certificate of registration.

## Maintenance

Federal trademarks must be maintained:
- **Between years 5–6**: File a Declaration of Use (Section 8)
- **At year 10**: File a Combined Declaration of Use and Application for Renewal (Section 8 & 9)
- **Every 10 years after**: Renew again

Failure to maintain = cancellation.

## Things to Know for Bot/Prompt Sellers

- If you sell bots as software tools, Class 9 likely applies.
- If you offer bots as a subscription or SaaS service, Class 42 likely applies.
- You may need to file in both classes depending on how you sell.
- "Intent to Use" applications are common for creators who haven't launched yet — you can lock in your name before you go live.

## Disclaimer

Trademark law is complex. A trademark attorney can run a professional clearance search (more thorough than TESS alone) and help you avoid costly mistakes. This document is orientation only — not legal advice.
